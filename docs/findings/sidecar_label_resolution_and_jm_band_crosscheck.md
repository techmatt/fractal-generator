# Sidecar-label contradiction — settled, consolidated, and the jm-band cross-check

**Date:** 2026-07-17 · **Scope:** read-only on the corpus. No thresholds changed, no
retrain, no manifest edits. Two code changes only, both to *readers*: registered the two
jm-band batches in the canonical label registry, and pointed the q3-rate report at that
canonical resolver.

---

## 1. Which finding was wrong

**`v6_training_coverage_audit.md` was right; `labeled_q3_rate_by_category.md` was wrong.**
The "jm3/4/5 revival label pass never happened" reading was the error — the pass did
happen and the labels are on disk.

The two documents disagreed on whether `mining_v3guided`, both `scale_2x2` batches,
`jm3_band`, `jm45_band`, and `julia_ladder_j0` carry human labels. Ground-truth from the
files:

- Every one of those batches has an **empty `scores.json` and zero in-row
  `label.score`** (verified per batch).
- Every one has a `labels/*.json` sidecar in the repo-root `labels/` dir, keyed by
  `image_id`, that **joins 100% to the batch's `images.jsonl`** (0 unjoinable across all
  of them): `julia_ladder_j0`=1000, `mining`=450, `scale_2x2`=600,
  `scale_controlled_2x2`=600, `jm3_band`=58, `jm45_band`=71.

So the audit's counts (jm3_band = 57, jm45_band = 68 *locations*, after max-over-crops
dedup of the 58/71 crops) are correct, and the q3-rate report's "UNLABELED / contribute
nothing" was a reader defect.

### Root cause — a fourth label reader that rolled its own loading

`q3_rate_by_category.py` resolved a label as *in-row `label.score` **else** the batch's
own `scores.json`* — and never looked at the repo-root `labels/*.json` sidecars. That
silently drops **2779 crop-labels** (the only home of which is a sidecar) and, worst,
erased the entire 1000-location J0 Julia ladder from the julia rate.

A correct, proven reader already existed: `tools/corpus/label_store.resolve_score`
(merged `label.score` **else** the registered sidecar joined by `image_id`), consumed by
`corpus_reader.iter_labeled` (the trainer's version-blind view) and
`query_sampler.LocationPool.from_corpus` (the location universe). That path is what makes
`julia_ladder_j0`'s 1000 locations land in v5→v6, reconciling to the manifest.

**Consolidated onto it (no fourth reader).** `q3_rate_by_category.py` now calls
`label_store.resolve_score`.

### The registry itself was incomplete — the deeper bug

`label_store.SIDECAR_LABELS` registered only `julia_ladder_j0`, `mining`, and the two
`scale` batches. **`jm3_band` and `jm45_band` were never registered** — they were labeled
2026-07-11/12, after the registry was last touched. So the "canonical" reader *also*
undercounted these two: `corpus_reader` reported them 0/58 and 0/71.

Registering them (`2026-07-11_jm3_band_v1` → `jm3_band_v1.json`, `2026-07-12_jm45_band_v1`
→ `jm45_band_v1.json`) fixes **every** canonical consumer at once — `corpus_reader`
(6877 → 7006 labeled crops), `query_sampler`, and `atlas/guard.py` all route through the
same `resolve_score`. `blindspot` and `prospect` also have repo-root sidecars but their
labels are already merged in-row, so `resolve_score` finds them without registration
(registry means "sidecar-**only**").

### Who else undercounts

The label *census/training* consumers that turn a corpus row into a label are exactly:
`corpus_reader` (trainer view), `query_sampler` (location pool), `atlas/guard.py`, and —
now fixed — `q3_rate_by_category.py`. The first three already route through
`resolve_score`, so **the single registry edit repairs all of them**; none needs a
separate patch. Every *other* file that reads a `scores.json` is a batch-builder or
merge/QA tool (`merge_scores.py`, `build_*_batch.py`, `gather_select.py`, the training
scripts that consume a frozen manifest, etc.), not a from-scratch label census, so they
are not in the undercount class. Net: the undercount was confined to the one rolled-its-own
reader plus the two unregistered batches, and both are now closed.

`labeled_q3_rate_by_category.md` is **superseded in place** (regenerated by the fixed
script; it now carries a `CORRECTED 2026-07-17` banner naming the error). Headline deltas,

| category | was | now |

| julia | 87 | **1087** (87 gather + 1000 J0) |
| julia_multibrot3 | 75 | 163 |

| **all locations** | 3624 | **5713** |

The corrected **all = 5713 reconciles exactly** to the v6 coverage audit's labeled-location
count, and julia / julia_multibrot\* match the audit row-for-row — an independent
cross-validation that the reader is now correct. (One category nuance, footnoted in the

`render.fractal_type` and fold into `mandelbrot`; nets to zero at the mandelbrot-group
level, never touches julia.)

### The verdict "only mandelbrot has an unbiased draw" — revised, not overturned

`julia_ladder_j0` is 1000 J0 Julia locations, so julia is no longer *unlabeled* here — but
it is **not an unbiased base rate**. Its regime (`tools/julia_ladder/build_j0.py`):

- Julia `c` values are the **label-2/3 (good) Mandelbrot centers** from the v4 manifest
  (893 raw → 618 deduped neighborhoods) — the parameter plane is pre-selected on
  already-good Mandelbrot locations.
- Each seed spawns systematic center-zoom rungs (`fw = 3.0/4`, `3.0/8`) **and**
  `guided-descend` descent rungs (descent selection), all v4-scored, then
  stratified-sampled to 1000.

So it is a **systematic zoom ladder conditioned on already-good `c`, with a
descent-selected half** — positively biased toward good Julia. Its q3 rate (loc-level
0.227) is an over-estimate, not a base rate. **Julia's true unbiased rate was not
available all along.** The honest position stands: mandelbrot's `loose0` is still the only
quasi-unbiased labeled draw; the correction is that the other families are *labeled but
biased*, not *unlabeled*.

## 3. The 125 jm-band labels as a cross-check — the payoff, and its limits

`jm3_band` (57 locs) + `jm45_band` (68 locs) sample the revival band the 83-crop stage-1
batch was built to probe. **But the machine-score axis of the cross-check is OFF**, exactly
as the task anticipated:

- **Not v6.** Every row carries `scorer_version = None`, so `corpus_common.is_v6_decoded`
  returns **False** — these are v5-vintage. Worse, they don't store `p_good` at all: the
  only machine number is `k3` (raw E[ord] ∈ [0,2]). Stage-1 stored genuine **v6 `p_good`**
  (`scorer_version="v6"`, Gate-2 parity max|Δ|=1e-4). **You cannot compare a v5 `k3` to
  stage-1's v6 `p_good`, nor to `t_good = 0.30` (a `p_good` operating point).** Do not pool
  the scores.
- **Different presentation.** Band crops are **1280×720 ss4** with **39 / 46 distinct
  palettes**; stage-1 was **640×360 ss2, single `twilight_shifted`**. So even the human
  labels are on a different geometry *and* varied palette — a caveat on comparing the human
  rates too, not just the machine scores.
- **Band definition.** Every band row is `decoded_class = 2` — the machine's "okay"
  class, which sits **below** the class-3 (good) decode line. By construction these are
  machine-sub-good locations.

### What the cross-check *does* deliver — human labels, no score comparability needed

The machine put all 125 in its **not-good** class. The human disagrees, hard:

| batch | machine class | human q3 (loc-level) | human q3 rate |
|---|---|---:|---:|
| jm3_band | all `decoded_class=2` | 26 / 57 | **0.46** |
| jm45_band | all `decoded_class=2` | 56 / 68 | **0.82** |

**This independently replicates — and strengthens — stage-1's headline.** Stage-1 found
the julia q3 mass sits *below* `t_good=0.30` and called the gate "too tight." The band
batches confirm it from the other side: **46% (jm3) and 82% (jm45) of the machine's
sub-good locations are human-q3.** Two independent passes, different scorer version and
different presentation, now agree that the v6 decode gate discards a large fraction of real
q3s in jm3/4/5. jm45 is the sharp case — four of every five machine-"okay" jm4/jm5
locations are actually good.

### The ranking-within-band check — suggestive, not conclusive

Ranking the stored **`k3`** (v5-vintage, *not* v6 `p_good`) against the human label,
inside each band:

| batch | AUC(q3 vs rest) | Spearman(k3, score) |
|---|---:|---:|
| jm3_band | 0.634 | +0.26 |
| jm45_band | 0.530 | +0.06 |

Neither shows real ordering power — jm45 is at chance, jm3 weak-positive — consistent with
stage-1's "`p_good` does not order quality in this regime" (stage-1 julia AUC 0.443). The
band batches do **not** reproduce stage-1's *below-chance* inversion, but that is not a
contradiction: it's a different score (v5 `k3` vs v6 `p_good`), a narrow band (`k3` ∈
1.14–1.45, all class 2, so range restriction attenuates), and a different presentation. The
defensible joint statement: **neither pass finds the machine score ordering quality inside
this band; the exact AUC is not comparable across the two because the score versions
differ.**

### Bottom line

- The audit was right; the q3-rate report (and the underlying registry) was wrong. Fixed
  by consolidating on `label_store.resolve_score` and completing its `SIDECAR_LABELS`
  registry — one reader, all consumers.
- Corrected q3 table reconciles to the audit's 5713; julia is labeled-but-biased, not a
  base rate.
- The jm-band cross-check **agrees with stage-1 on the thing that matters** — the v6 gate
  is dropping real jm3/4/5 q3s at scale (46% / 82% of machine-"okay" locations are
  human-good) — while its machine-score axis is not directly comparable (v5 `k3`, ss4
  varied-palette, vs stage-1's v6 `p_good` at ss2 twilight). Report it as two agreeing
  passes on the gate, one non-comparable axis on the ranking.
